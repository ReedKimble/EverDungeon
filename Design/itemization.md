# Itemization

The game utilizes a detailed and realistic web of resources and materials for creating items. Each material provides components of what become the aggregate properties of the items created with those components. The skill of the crafter and rating of their tools also contribute to the quality and value of the crafted item or component. This allows various skill levels to compound or cancel each other throughout a production process and makes for a lot more variety in the value of materials for trade.

## Resources

All common mineral and metal resources are available in the form of raw ores and crystals. Each can be refind into workable ingots or gemstones. Further production can produce metal bands, bolts, nails, clamps, chains, etc. or cut stones and lenses. Other minerals such as salts and organics such as coal can also be found.

Wood materials are rarer but still available in underground forests of low trunks and root and grottos exposed to the surface. They can also be scavanged from structures found while exploring dungeon areas.

Some magical resources exist as well such as "Mythril Ore" and "Mana Crystal" as examples.

## Value

Item value is determined by:

 * The quality and rarity of the resources used in each component
 * The craftsmanship of each component
 * The craftsmanship of the assembly

This provides a consistent value system for all items. Magic spells and effects are similarly constructed from skill based components and casting constituants so spell scrolls and enchanted items can also have a calculated value.

### Coin

Coin is divided into currencies:

 * Copper: 1 base unit of currency. A loaf of bread is 2c. A hot meal at an inn is 7-12c. A room for the night for one person might be 20-40c.
 * Silver: 100c. A sturdy steel shortsword might be 4s. A set of studded or banded armor might cost 16-24s. 
 * Gold: 50s. Two horses and wagon might cost 1g20s. A rare item might be worth a 1g or more.
 * Platinum: 10g. A small house might cost 1p5g. A magic relic might be worth 1p or more.
